Performance summaryClaude

OVERALL

Q2 quarter-to-date is 41,457 views across 222 platform postings, with 11 YouTube subscribers gained. YouTube Shorts carries the channel: 22,218 views (54% of the total) off 47 posts. The pattern is clear. Volume and retention are healthy, but discovery is the bottleneck. Average view duration is up this quarter while click-through and new-viewer reach have softened, so the front door (titles and thumbnails) is now the lever, not the watch experience.


PER PLATFORM

YouTube Shorts: The workhorse at 473 avg views per post. Retention is strong; the addressable drop is CTR. Title shape is the proven fix (see EXP7).

TikTok: 210 avg views over 51 posts. Steady reach, the most consistent of the social channels.

Instagram: 125 avg views but the highest engagement rate. A distribution ceiling, not a content problem. Trial reels (EXP2, EXP6) are the chosen workaround.

Facebook: 28 avg views. Supporting channel only; post for hygiene, not reach.


CROSS-PLATFORM VARIANCE

The same script regularly performs many times better on TikTok than Instagram. Review and decision-page scripts skew hard to TikTok; Mosseri and Google-strategy angles skew to Instagram. Pick the platform to the script.


EXPERIMENTS

Eight Q2 experiments are now logged. Confirmed wins: EXP2 (the Ranked List hook beat four others by 19% on cold IG audiences) and EXP5 (a re-edit of an identical script drove 73% more views on 2.6 times the retention, proving the edit is a separable lever). EXP7 validated that stakes-shaped titles beat abstract ones and is now enforced in the CE4 title gate.


RECOMMENDATIONS
1. Fix YouTube titles first. CTR is the single biggest addressable drop. Apply the EXP7 stakes-title shape to every Short.
2. Lean on the winning hook pattern. Weight EXP2's Ranked List plus visceral dichotomy higher in hook generation by default.
3. Reallocate Instagram effort. Post for hygiene, route real testing through trial reels, and do not produce IG-specific content while reach is gated.
4. Ship EXP3 (head-pop) this week as platform experiment number two, matched against a standard-hook control.

The insight

Longer, searchable videos converted subscribers ~70x better per view.

Q4's long-form tutorials turned 246 views into 3 subscribers (1.2 per 100). Q2's shorts turned 6,356 views into 1 (0.02 per 100). Shorts are clearly the reach engine. Leaning into longer, searchable content could be one way to encourage more subscribers, worth a test.

The through-line

The time the engine saved is the time that grew the channels.

The pipeline cut production from most of a day to under an hour. That freed-up time went into testing, hooks and analysis, which is what pushed views up roughly 29x on Smash Balloon's YouTube, 26x on WPChat, and nearly doubled Smash Balloon's TikTok reach quarter over quarter. The remaining gap is subscriber and engagement conversion, and the data points to a few levers worth testing next. The build is done and the impact is measured. Next is a written walkthrough so the team can run it too, the move from Adaptive to Transformative.
