Performance summaryClaude

OVERALL

Q2 shows strong volume growth — 37 short-form posts across the Big 3 (+106% vs Q1's 18), driving 10,565 views (+80%). YouTube Shorts is carrying the channel: 74% of Big 3 views come from just 27% of the posts, and avg views/short is up 25% YoY (782 vs 624). The concerns: Shorts CTR collapsed from 3.84% to 1.54%, TikTok avg plays halved (184 vs 325), and quarter subscribers are trailing (7 vs Q1's 26 total). Publishing cadence is the clear win; cut-through per post is the clear issue.


PER PLATFORM

YouTube Shorts: The workhorse — 782 avg views, 8 of top 10 posts, beating Q1 on reach. But CTR fell 60% — thumbnail and first-3-seconds hook work is the biggest lever here.

TikTok: More volume but less per-post reach (avg 184 vs 325). Engagement rate actually ROSE (5.28% vs 4.2%) — the existing audience is more active, but the algorithm isn't pushing reach to new viewers.

Instagram: Highest engagement rate (27.8%) but tiny absolute reach (25 avg views/reel). This is a distribution problem, not a content problem.

Facebook: Avg views +488% but from near-zero (5.6 → 33). Supporting channel only; the jump is mostly a volume artefact.


CROSS-PLATFORM VARIANCE

The same video regularly performs 5-40× better on TikTok than Instagram. "Almost half of all consumers use TikTok as a search engine" got 196 plays on TikTok vs 5 views on Instagram — identical caption, same day. Likely causes: Instagram's algorithm is gating distribution to small business accounts (text-heavy Reels are deprioritized), while TikTok's For You page still rewards small accounts with business content. Meta-content (videos ABOUT Instagram, Facebook, TikTok) consistently outperforms platform-native topics — audiences click curiosity, not "how-to".


RECOMMENDATIONS
1. A/B test YouTube thumbnails and first-3-second hooks — CTR regression is the single biggest addressable drop (3.84% → 1.54%).
2. Reallocate Instagram effort — post for hygiene but don't produce IG-specific content. Views/production hour is 10-40× higher on TikTok and YouTube.
3. Lean into meta-content — Facebook strategy (924 avg views), Instagram strategy (329 avg), Reviews (154 avg) all outperform. Avoid TikTok-strategy topics (24 avg) — even TikTok's audience doesn't want meta-TikTok content.
